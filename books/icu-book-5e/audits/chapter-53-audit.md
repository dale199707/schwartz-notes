| 1 | Are rescuer safety, PPE and decontamination prioritized before routine resuscitation-area entry? | Chemical scenes require source control, PPE and removal/containment of contaminated clothing to avoid secondary casualties. | Decontamination mentioned after diagnosis. | SUPPORTED | Moved scene/staff safety to the first principle. | [AHA special circumstances](https://cpr.heart.org/en/resuscitation-science/cpr-and-ecc-guidelines/adult-and-pediatric-special-circumstances-of-resuscitation) |
| 2 | Is CO diagnosed without reliance on pulse oximetry, PaO2 or a single COHb severity threshold? | Co-oximetry confirms exposure; prior oxygen/time lowers COHb and clinical severity may not correlate with the value. | COHb >25% treated as a near-binary severity rule. | SUPPORTED | Added measurement limitations and clinical organ assessment. | [CDC CO guidance](https://www.cdc.gov/carbon-monoxide/hcp/clinical-guidance/index.html) |
| 3 | Is hyperbaric oxygen consultation multifactorial and is delayed neurologic follow-up included? | Neurologic/cardiac injury, acidosis, unconsciousness, COHb >25–30% and pregnancy inform HBO; delayed sequelae require warning/follow-up. | HBO overly threshold-based; follow-up absent. | SUPPORTED | Added multifactorial consultation and delayed neuropsychiatric surveillance. | [CDC CO guidance](https://www.cdc.gov/carbon-monoxide/hcp/clinical-guidance/index.html) |
| 4 | Is smoke-related cyanide treated promptly with a safe first-line antidote? | Severe compatible smoke exposure can justify empiric hydroxocobalamin; nitrites may worsen oxygen delivery in concurrent CO poisoning. | Hydroxocobalamin and nitrite kit presented as equivalent. | SUPPORTED | Preferred hydroxocobalamin and added nitrite, lab and dialysis-machine caveats. | [CHEMM](https://chemm.hhs.gov/countermeasure_hydroxocobalamin.htm) |
| 5 | Are toxic-alcohol gaps interpreted over time and is fomepizole not delayed for a send-out level? | Osmolar gap may be early and anion gap late; normal gaps do not exclude, and credible severe suspicion warrants immediate ADH blockade. | Gap progression and normal-gap limits incomplete. | SUPPORTED | Added time-dependent gaps and empiric fomepizole. | [EXTRIP methanol](https://www.extrip-workgroup.org/methanol) |
| 6 | Are methanol/EG manifestations, cofactors and dialysis indications distinguished? | Methanol causes visual/CNS injury; EG causes calcium/renal injury; severe acidosis, organ injury and defined levels/gaps guide ECTR. | Shared pathway lacked organ-specific triggers. | SUPPORTED | Added distinct syndromes, cofactors and EXTRIP-directed HD. | [EXTRIP ethylene glycol](https://www.extrip-workgroup.org/ethylene-glycol) |
| 7 | Is isopropanol separated from methanol/EG? | Isopropanol usually causes ketosis without AG acidosis and receives supportive care; routine fomepizole prolongs clearance. | Isopropanol omitted. | SUPPORTED | Added diagnostic and treatment distinction. | [ACEP toxic alcohols](https://www.acep.org/toxicology/newsroom/Oct2020/toxic-alcohols) |
| 8 | Is cholinesterase poisoning treated to respiratory endpoints with decontamination and early oxime consideration? | Atropine should be rapidly doubled every 3–5 minutes until secretions/bronchospasm and perfusion improve; pupil/heart rate are not endpoints. | Atropine present but endpoints and staff safety were incomplete. | SUPPORTED | Added rapid doubling, respiratory endpoints, pralidoxime uncertainty and delayed syndromes. | [AHA special circumstances](https://cpr.heart.org/en/resuscitation-science/cpr-and-ecc-guidelines/adult-and-pediatric-special-circumstances-of-resuscitation) |
| 9 | Are methemoglobinemia diagnosis and methylene-blue hazards covered? | Saturation gap/chocolate blood and co-oximetry support diagnosis; methylene blue needs G6PD and serotonergic-drug caution. | Major antidote-relevant omission. | SUPPORTED | Added recognition, usual dose and safety escalation. | [AHA special circumstances](https://cpr.heart.org/en/resuscitation-science/cpr-and-ecc-guidelines/adult-and-pediatric-special-circumstances-of-resuscitation) |
| 10 | Are irritant gases and hydrocarbons managed supportively without routine ineffective therapy? | Solubility predicts early vs delayed injury; oxygen/airway/bronchodilator support is primary, while prophylactic antibiotics/steroids and charcoal for hydrocarbons are not routine. | Fixed observation and nonspecific treatment were overgeneralized. | SUPPORTED | Added gas-specific timing, symptom-based observation and hydrocarbon aspiration cautions. | [ATSDR chlorine](https://wwwn.cdc.gov/TSP/MMG/MMGDetails.aspx?mmgid=198&toxid=36) |

## Corrections and residual uncertainty

Reframed CO/HBO decisions around organ injury rather than one COHb threshold, made hydroxocobalamin the preferred severe-smoke cyanide antidote, added time-dependent toxic-alcohol gaps, distinguished isopropanol, and supplied organophosphate respiratory endpoints, methemoglobinemia and delayed-gas-injury safeguards. HBO selection, empiric hydroxocobalamin thresholds and pralidoxime benefit remain areas of limited or heterogeneous evidence.

## Re-audit conclusion

All ten questions are `SUPPORTED` after revision. This 10/10 score is an editorial threshold, not a guarantee of clinical correctness; chemical exposures require responder safety, product-specific poison-center/hazmat input and patient-specific antidote/extracorporeal decisions.
